About

About me

Curious. Caffeinated. Occasionally paranoid—for a reason.

Digital security isn't a clean-cut game of heroes and villains. It's a messy, fascinating ecosystem where logic, persistence, and a sense of humour make all the difference. I've got the technical background—yes, degrees in computer science, maths, and education—but it's the lifelong curiosity and people-first mindset that drive the work I do.

Why I do this

Security is never static. Neither am I.

Vulnerabilities evolve, and so must we. I don’t claim to know it all, but I stay curious, keep learning, and connect the dots with context and clarity. I help people spot the risks, understand the threats, and build resilience—even when budgets are tight or time is shorter than you'd like.

What you get

Logic, empathy, and support—with a side of dry wit.

Whether you're running a SOC, wrangling SIEMs, or just trying to get a handle on basic digital hygiene, I can help. From teaching security concepts to supporting threat modelling efforts, I work with individuals and not-for-profits to make cybersecurity more accessible and effective.

My style

Low budget. High signal. No buzzwords.

Working in this field means living with a healthy level of paranoia. But constant tension doesn’t help anyone think straight. I use humour as oxygen—it cuts through fear and keeps things human. Security work should be clear, not cryptic. Collaborative, not condescending.
