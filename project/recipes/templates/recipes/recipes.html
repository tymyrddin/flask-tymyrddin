Security doesn’t have to sound like sorcery

I'm Nienke Fokma — some know me as Nina Barzh — and I help people make sense of the digital mess we've all inherited.

Security shouldn't feel like an elite club for hoodie-clad cryptographers. It should be understandable, usable, and above all, relevant. That’s why I work with NGOs, small businesses, and curious individuals who want more than just checklists — they want clarity. And maybe the odd firewall that doesn’t implode when someone blinks at it.

Whether I’m unpacking how attackers actually think, untangling a phishing lure, or translating policy-speak into plain English, I do security as conversation — not sermon. No jargon unless it's ironic. No snake oil. Just practical threat modelling, low-budget resilience, and advice that assumes you’ve got more to do than memorise CVEs.

My approach? Grounded. Informed. Not afraid to name the systemic flaws behind your inbox disasters.

My goal? Replacing anxiety with agency — one plain-spoken, context-rich explanation at a time. Because in a world built on digital duct tape, your best defence isn’t more tech. It’s understanding what’s really going on behind the screen.
